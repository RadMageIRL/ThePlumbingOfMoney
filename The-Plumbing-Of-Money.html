The plumbing
of money.

Banking isn't one network — it's stacked layers of messaging and settlement that almost never move the same way. An instruction races one way as data; the actual value settles separately, often hours later, as a balance change on a central bank's ledger. This maps the two channels — domestically in the US, across borders, and worldwide. Tap any node for what it does.

How a US payment settles

USD · Federal Reserve as the floor

Read it bottom-up. People and firms send instructions to their banks (cyan). Banks hand those to a rail. Every rail ultimately settles by moving central-bank reserves at the Federal Reserve (gold) — the one asset every US bank trusts as final.

Sending money abroad

USD → EUR · the message/value split, exposed

The hard truth of cross-border payments: money never leaves the country. A SWIFT message travels (cyan); the value is settled by adjusting correspondent accounts banks hold with each other, with each currency's leg settling inside its own domestic system (gold). Follow one payment left-to-right.

The world's settlement web

Central banks · one messaging fabric · FX glue

Each currency has its own central bank and real-time settlement system (gold). One cooperative messaging fabric — SWIFT — overlays nearly all of them (cyan). FX trades between currencies are settled simultaneously by CLS so neither side pays first. Above it all, the BIS sets the rules (dotted).

Schematic, not exhaustive. Simplifies netting, intraday credit, and prefunding mechanics for legibility.
Channels: message ≠ settlement. That distinction is the whole point.
